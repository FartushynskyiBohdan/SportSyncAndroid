# User Stories And Example Survey

This file is written as an **appendix**, not as fabricated research results. It gives you a defensible set of user stories and an example survey instrument you can reference in the proposal.

## Core User Stories

- As a runner who trains several times a week, I want to see another person’s sport and training frequency quickly so I can judge lifestyle compatibility before messaging.
- As a gym-focused user, I want onboarding to capture my main sport, experience level, and goals so the matches feel more relevant than on a general dating app.
- As a new user, I want a simple swipe-based discover screen so the app feels familiar and low-friction from the first session.
- As a matched user, I want messaging to be directly connected to the match flow so I can move from interest to conversation without searching through menus.
- As a safety-conscious user, I want block, report, and account-control options so I can trust the platform more than an unmoderated social app.
- As an athlete with a busy routine, I want a mobile interface that is fast, clear, and usable with one hand while commuting or between training sessions.

## Example Survey Goals

The survey should test whether the concept solves a real problem for active users.

Key themes:

- whether sport identity matters in romantic matching
- whether users would disclose training level and frequency
- whether a sport-focused niche app feels more relevant than a general dating app
- whether the mobile flow is easy to understand

## Example Survey Questions

- What is your age range?
- How often do you participate in sport or structured exercise each week?
- Have you ever used a dating app before?
- When evaluating a potential match, how important is lifestyle compatibility?
- How important is shared interest in sport or fitness to you?
- Would you be comfortable listing your main sport on a dating profile?
- Would you be comfortable listing your training frequency or ability level?
- Which is more appealing: a general dating app or a niche app for active people?
- Which feature would matter most to you: discover swiping, shared-sport filters, messaging, or profile depth?
- Would you prefer matches based on sport compatibility, location, or relationship goals?
- What concerns would you have about an athlete-focused dating app?
- What would make you trust the app enough to use it regularly?

## Example Open Questions

- Describe the kind of person you would expect to meet on SportSync.
- What information would help you decide whether to like or pass on a profile?
- What would make this app feel different from Tinder or Bumble?
- What would make the app feel safe and respectful?

## How To Reference This In The Proposal

You can use wording like this:

> To support market validation, I drafted user stories and a short survey instrument focused on sport identity, training frequency, safety, and willingness to use a niche matching platform. The purpose was to test whether athletes value compatibility signals that are usually secondary in mainstream dating apps.

## Honest Limitation

If you did not run a real survey, say so clearly. A strong sentence is:

> Due to time constraints, the survey instrument was designed as a validation framework rather than executed at scale, but it still helped define the most important assumptions behind the prototype.
